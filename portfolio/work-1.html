I wask asked the question: can we grow our customer base via our Suppliers (aka Pros)?

Over 3 weeks, we discovered that the ability for Setter to grow through it's Supplier base is small, and crossed the growth-lever off the to-do list.

I took a systematic approach to reach this conclusion. Step one was understanding the fragmentation of Setter's supplier base. Once I discovered that ~27 Pros represented >50% of jobs, I decided to reach out to all 27 Suppliers to get their perspective on becoming referal agents.

In the process, (i) I documented feedback on Setter and clustered pain points to recommend approaches for Setter to improve the experience for Pros, and (ii) triaged the top Pros into evangelists, happy critics, and flight risks, so Setter knew where to focus Supplier health efforts. The result was several product and process recommendations, some of which were implemented immediately (e.g. Pros complained that communicaiton was slow, especially frustrating when on site with customer but must communicate through Setter. In order to alleviate pain point, Setter Home Managers are now calling Pros 1-hour before job to notify them that they're on call while Pros are on site.)

Of the 27 Pros I reached out to, roughly 5 engaged in a referal program. A team at Setter and I designed flyers, referal codes, and distributed them to the 5 Pros. We iterated several times on the flyer for Pros and their customers incorporating urgency and escalating incentives even beyond viable cost-of-customer acquisition. We managed to get ~5 referals but only with heavy handholding from us (indeed, we were on site with the Pros for 2 of these).

When it comes to refering Setter, Pros can do one of three things: (1) nothing, (2) inform when asked, (3) proactively pitch Setter. It turns out that Pros are unmotivated to proactively pitch Setter because while there is a small upside for them, doing so is non-core to their business and they worry that pitching Setter is a risk to their repeat business because (i) they appear salesy, and/or (ii) Setter may in the future refer ongoing business from that customer elsewhere . Nevertheless, all Pros should be informing Setter when asked. The 5 Pros engaged will now do so, and going forwards, it was recommended that each Pro gets setup with a trackable referal link and associated leave behinds such that they're able to easily inform customers about Setter and get compensated for doing so when the opportunity arises.
