About Rosey

As a kid, Bryan decided he was either to become Superman or Inspector Gadget. After jumping off the second floor balcony and discovering he really can't fly, he settled on Inspector Gadget.


That led him to studying Mechanical Engineer at McGill where he graduated 3rd in class, then to joining guru entreprenuer Jonathan Goodman as employee #2 at Knight Therapeutics (now valued at $1.2B), then to launch a technology venture of his own that was angel-funded part of Canada's Next 36 in Toronto.

Realizing that fueling the robotic future involved being strategic, it led him to McKinsey, where he served a large variety of Fortune 500 clients across industries (incl. mining, aerospace, consumer electronics, private equity) on a wide variety of topics (strategy, innovation, product development, operations, and due dilligence).

Wanting to have more control and freedom over his work, it's led him to launch Rosey, a platform upon which he supports companies working to build the future.
